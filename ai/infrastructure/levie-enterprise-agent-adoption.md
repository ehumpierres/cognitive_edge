# Enterprise Agent Adoption: Field Report

**Source**: Aaron Levie (Box CEO) on X  
**Date**: 2026-04-12  
**Topic**: Ground-truth observations from meetings with IT/AI leaders at large enterprises (banking, media, retail, healthcare, consulting, tech, sports)

---

## Core Observation

> "We're moving from chat era of AI to agents that use tools, process data, and start to execute real work in the enterprise."

Enterprises are evolving from "let a thousand flowers bloom" to **targeted automation** applied to specific workflows.

---

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Change Management is the Bottleneck

Most workflows aren't set up to just drop agents in. Enterprises need massive help (internal + partners) to drive adoption.

**Example pattern:** One company has a **head of AI in every business unit** that rolls up to a central team, just to keep functions coordinated.

### 2. Tokenmaxxing: Compute Budgeting is Real

Most enterprises operate with strict OpEx budgets locked in for the year ahead. They're having **real trade-off discussions** on how to budget for tokens.

**Emerging patterns:**
- "Shark tank" style pitching for compute budget
- Hierarchy of needs to ration compute to best use-cases
- Token economics is a board-level conversation

### 3. Legacy Systems Block Agent Access

Enterprises are dealing with **decades of on-prem or cloud-but-not-modernized systems**. Agents can't easily tap into these data sources in a unified way yet.

**Current priority:** How to modernize fragmented systems so agents can access them.

### 4. Agents Create New Capabilities, Not Job Cuts

> "Most companies are *not* talking about replacing jobs due to agents."

Major use-cases are things the company **wasn't able to do before or couldn't prioritize**:
- Software upgrades
- Automating back-office processes that constrained other workflows
- Processing large documents for business/client insights

**More emphasis on ways to make money vs. cut costs.**

### 5. Headless Software is Non-Negotiable

> "Enterprises need to be able to ensure all of their software works across any set of agents they choose. They will kick out vendors that don't make this technically or economically easy."

Vendors without agent-friendly APIs will be replaced.

### 6. Standardization is Impossible Right Now

The pace of innovation is a blessing and curse. No one wants to get locked into the wrong architecture.

**Result:** Companies realize they're in a **multi-agent world**, which means **interoperability becomes paramount** across systems.

### 7. Everyone is Working More, Not Less

> "AI is not causing anyone to do less work right now... teams are the busiest they've ever been."

The work is shifting, not disappearing.

---

## Meta Observation: Technical Diffusion

> "Despite Silicon Valley's sense that AI has made hard things easy, the most powerful ways to use agents is more 'technical' than prior eras of software. Skills, MCP, CLIs, etc. may be simple concepts for tech, but in the real world these are all esoteric concepts that will require technical people to help bring to life in the enterprise."

**Implication #1:** Diffusion will take real work and time.

**Implication #2:** Engineering jobs estimation is totally off.
> "Engineers may not be 'writing' software, but they will certainly be the ones to setup and operate the systems that actually automate most work in the enterprise."

---

## Key Insights for Strategy

| Observation | Implication |
|-------------|-------------|
| Chat → Agents | The interface isn't the product anymore; the automation is |
| Change management | Budget for organizational change, not just technology |
| Tokenmaxxing | Token economics is strategic, not operational |
| Legacy systems | Data access is the real blocker, not model capability |
| New capabilities > job cuts | Pitch agents as revenue enablers, not cost cutters |
| Headless software | If your product doesn't have agent APIs, you'll be replaced |
| Multi-agent interop | Design for a world where you're one of many agents |
| Technical diffusion | The "AI makes it easy" narrative doesn't apply in enterprise |
